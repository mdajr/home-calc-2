Loan scenarios

One card per lender quote. Enter points as % or $ (they stay in sync; negative = credit priced into the rate). Paste the sheet's quoted P&I to verify you transcribed the rate and loan right. Prepaid interest is computed per loan from its rate.
Where to find these numbers on a lender's sheet
Official Loan Estimate (3-page form): Rate and Sheet P&I — page 1 "Loan Terms". Points $ — Section A, the "…% of Loan Amount (Points)" line. Lender fees — the rest of A (underwriting, origination) plus all of B (appraisal, credit report, MERS, flood cert, tax service, processing). Credits — the "Lender Credits" line. Escrow — Section G total (lenders demand different months; overrides the shared default for this loan only). Skip sections C, E, F, H — title, transfer/recording taxes, prepaids, and realtor fees are modeled once in the shared closing-costs panel so lenders compare apples-to-apples, using our own title agent's numbers. (A lender's own "cash to close" can also differ for non-pricing reasons, e.g. an earnest deposit they didn't credit.)

Rate menus (e.g. Chase): enter Rate and the shown P&I as Sheet P&I. Menus rarely split out points, but the difference in "estimated total fees" between two rows is pure rate pricing: treat the lowest-fee row as ≈0 points and enter each other row's Points $ as its fees minus that baseline (tiny error: prepaid interest varies slightly with rate). Leave lender fees at the default unless the menu itemizes them.
